    Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

    This a summary from Griffiths textbook

    The potential energy for a classical harmonic oscillator takes the form:

    $$V(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^{2}$$

    Equivalently

    $$V(x) = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^{2}x^{2}, \quad \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

    Thus, the Time-Independent Schrodinger Equation (TISE) reads:

    $$\frac{-\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{d^2\psi(x)}{dx^2} + V(x)\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$

    and so we need to solve:

    $$\overbrace{\frac{-\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{d^2\psi(x)}{dx^2}}^{T(x)} + \overbrace{\frac{m\omega^2 x^2}{2}}^{V(x)}\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$

    Recall:

    $$T(x) = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}$$

    and $$p = mv$$ so

    $$T(x) = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2} = \frac{m^{2}v^{2}}{2m} = \frac{p^{2}}{2m}$$

    TISE now becomes:

    $$\frac{p^2}{2m}\psi(x) + \frac{m\omega^2 x^2}{2}\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$

    $$\iff$$

    $$\overbrace{\frac{1}{2m}\left(p^2 + (m\omega x)^2\right)}^{\mathcal{H}}\psi(x) = E\psi(x)$$

    So we wish to factor the Hamiltonian as:

    $$\mathcal{H} = \frac{1}{2m}\left(p^2 + (m\omega x)^2\right)$$

    Classically, this would be easy, we simply recall:

    $$(u^2 + v^2) = (u + iv)(u - iv)$$

    But we have to quantize and upgrade to operators first:

    $$\hat{\mathcal{H}} = \frac{1}{2m}\left(\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega \hat{x})^2\right)$$

    and operators do not commute, nonetheless, we can define:

    $$\hat{a}_{\pm} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\hbar m\omega}}\left(\mp i\hat{p} + m\omega\hat{x}\right)$$

    Thus,

    $$\begin{aligned} \hat{a}_-\hat{a}_+ &= \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}(i\hat{p} + m\omega\hat{x})(-i\hat{p} + m\omega\hat{x}) \\ &= \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}\left[(i\hat{p})(-i\hat{p}) + (i\hat{p})(m\omega\hat{x}) + (m\omega\hat{x})(-i\hat{p}) + (m\omega\hat{x})^2\right] \\ &= \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}\left[\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega\hat{x})^2 - im\omega(\hat{x}\hat{p} - \hat{p}\hat{x})\right] \quad \leftarrow \text{commutator} \\ &= \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}\left[\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega\hat{x})^2\right] - \frac{i}{2\hbar}[\hat{x}, \hat{p}] \end{aligned}$$

    Now, we know that $$\hat{x}$$ and $$\hat{p}$$ are conjugate variables, so by definition,

    [\hat{x}, \hat{p}] = i\hbar

    and we can now rewrite our equation as

    $$\hat{a}_-\hat{a}_+ = \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}\left[\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega\hat{x})^2\right] - \frac{i}{2\hbar}(i\hbar)$$

    $$= \frac{1}{2\hbar m\omega}\left[\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega\hat{x})^2\right] + \frac{1}{2}$$

    $$= \frac{1}{\hbar\omega}\overbrace{\left(\frac{1}{2m}\left[\hat{p}^2 + (m\omega\hat{x})^2\right]\right)}^{\mathcal{H}} + \frac{1}{2}$$

    $$= \frac{1}{\hbar\omega}\mathcal{H} + \frac{1}{2} \quad \iff \quad \mathcal{H} = \hbar\omega\left(\hat{a}_-\hat{a}_+ - \frac{1}{2}\right)$$

    Similarly,

    $$\hat{a}_+\hat{a}_- = \frac{1}{\hbar\omega}\mathcal{H} - \frac{1}{2} \quad \iff \quad \mathcal{H} = \hbar\omega\left(\hat{a}_+\hat{a}_- + \frac{1}{2}\right)$$

    Thus,

    $$\mathcal{H} = \hbar\omega\left(\hat{a}_{\pm}\hat{a}_{\mp} \pm \frac{1}{2}\right)$$

    SE now reads:

    $$\hbar\omega\left(\hat{a}_{\pm}\hat{a}_{\mp} \pm \frac{1}{2}\right)\psi = E\psi$$

    $$\text{Lemma: } \mathcal{H}\psi = E\psi \implies \mathcal{H}(\hat{a}_+\psi) = (E + \hbar\omega)(\hat{a}_+\psi), \quad \mathcal{H}(\hat{a}_-\psi) = (E - \hbar\omega)(\hat{a}_-\psi)$$

    $$\text{Lemma: } \hat{a}_+\psi_n = \sqrt{n+1}\;\psi_{n+1} \quad \text{and} \quad \hat{a}_-\psi_n = \sqrt{n}\;\psi_{n-1}$$

    Also note,

    $$\hat{x} = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{2m\omega}}(\hat{a}_+ - \hat{a}_-) \quad \text{and} \quad \hat{p} = i\sqrt{\frac{\hbar m\omega}{2}}(\hat{a}_+ - \hat{a}_-)$$

    Transmon Hamiltonian

    The transmon is a nonlinear oscillator. Its Hamiltonian in the charge basis is:

    $$\hat{H} = 4E_C\hat{n}^2 - E_J\cos\hat{\varphi}$$

    where $E_C$ is the charging energy, $E_J$ is the Josephson energy, $\hat{n}$ is the Cooper pair number operator, and $\hat{\varphi}$ is the superconducting phase. The transmon regime is defined by $E_J / E_C \gg 1$, which exponentially suppresses charge dispersion while only polynomially reducing anharmonicity.

    In the Fock (harmonic oscillator) approximation, we expand the cosine to fourth order:

    $$\hat{H} \approx \sqrt{8E_JE_C}\left(\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a} + \frac{1}{2}\right) - \frac{E_C}{12}\left(\hat{a} + \hat{a}^\dagger\right)^4$$

    The first term gives us equispaced levels at frequency $\omega_q = \sqrt{8E_JE_C} - E_C$, and the quartic correction introduces the anharmonicity $\alpha \approx -E_C$ that makes the transmon usable as a qubit.
